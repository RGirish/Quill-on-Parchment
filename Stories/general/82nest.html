Drizzle turned into a heavy downpour. The little girl in her teens was more anxious about the fledgling she saw in the daylight. It was a pleasant memory of that morn, the sight of four cute little birds, with their mouths wide open, expecting their mother’s recipe for their hunger, made her thrilled. The parent bird chirped to announce its arrival. Hurriedly she got down the stool on which she had climbed to peep into the nest.

Day break was pleasant with dew drops on the plant leaves. Spotting a small nest in the crotons plant in front of her house, Usha was very much excited. She always loved nests, a symbol of family, shelter and protection.

She had watched in Animal Planet, a TV channel, the construction of a well woven nest by the parent birds, which is the foundation for the beginning of their new family for that season. She was amazed by their plan for the construction of their nest, their home- a lovely, lively living place. They first weave a base with twigs collected from varied trees and plants. Inside the twig woven base, they lay soft materials like leaves and even cotton or such soft materials to support the tender skins of the new born. When she observed birds flying with twig or cotton in their beaks she was amazed.

The sight of the nest brought back the scenes from the TV program in her memory’s picture gallery. She became more curious. She wished to have an inner view of the nest. She first checked whether there was any bird of that particular nest roaming around, else she may perturb the new pair. She was appeased not to view any bird in sight. She went inside the hexagon shaped hall of her house. Brought a stool and silently placed it underneath the nest. Thrilled, she noiselessly climbed up the stool to have a view of the nest in the top angle. Oh! What a surprise. Her eyes opened wide to see four small beaks, rather big compared to the body, wide open ready to gulp anything their parent has brought for their breakfast. She was taken aback. She immediately got down the stool and hid herself behind the column to avoid visibility.

The parent birds, a pair of lovely tiny Bulbuls, from a distance sensed some change in the view of the plant and approached the nest swift. Usha was already in hideout. The birds were carrying something like worms in their beaks. She could view them drop the food they have brought inside their nest. Then they surveyed the area around their home to check the safety of their children. They no longer seemed comfortable. Has their children informed them about the new comer? They took turns now. A bird was roaming around while the other went far in search of food. When that bird returned, it took the vigilance while the watch keeping bird went to fetch the needs of their children.

Usha was running late for her college. She had to leave her guests half-way. She had to move fast to be in time for her M.A. classes. She was absorbed in her Literature classes throughout the day that she almost forgot her morning experience.

At night in her bed, she was musing over that day’s happening. A flash picture of the wide opened beaks in her mind brought her back to the morning’s happenings. She sharpened her ears to hear the drizzling sound of rain. It was dark outside, near the nest. The nest was away from the bulb that supplied light only to a small area in the outside verandah. She reached for the torch light and was standing near the front room’s window when she heard a heavy downpour of showers. The seasonal shower was unwelcomed by her today. She was worried about the safety of the fledglings.

She lit the torch and tried to view the nest which was almost of the same height as that of the window. She was shaken to see the view on the plant. A parent bird was patiently sheltering its kids with its feathers and getting exposed to the outburst of the nature. The small little parent Bulbul was shivering. But it sat steadily covering the whole nest with its wings and Usha recognized a proud expression on the parent bird.

She was not surprised for she had often tasted the parental protection throughout her childhood, till now. Birds are not exemptions in parenting. A parent is always a parent, be it a bird, animal or human. Parental love is selfless. It is unconditional love which expects nothing but the wellbeing of the child in turn.
