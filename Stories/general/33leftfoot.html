Disclaimer: although the title is a tribute to Christy brown and the brilliant portrayal of his life beyond cerebral palsy, by Daniel Day-Lewis in the title’s namesake drama; the following piece of work is more of a 2 am monologue about my weird paranoia. I believe all of us are entitled to one weird and unreasonable paranoia that shows us in the light of a schizophrenic. For instance my neighbor is always paranoid around balloons. They remind her of giant floating baby heads. Personally I think she watches too much of Phineas and Ferb but I guess what girl wouldn’t be freaked out by that. After all they have to give birth to babies. And then there is my brother who has this recent hatred towards chocolate doughnuts ever since he had a nightmare in which he was chased around a skateboard rink by a giant chocolate doughnut. It is not in the same league as the giant floating baby head, you know who runs away from a giant doughnut? You just pounce on that son of a gun and eat it. Still, you get where I am going.

I am a normal (definition of normal may vary from person to person), beer chugging, pizza loving, 20-something football addict who is not so surprisingly single. But mind you, I am not whining about being single. My last girlfriend was such a bed hog. She would hardly ever sleep on her half of the bed. She literally pushed me down the bed a couple of nights. And sorry if I seem like a territorial bed maniac but I had to break up with her. Oh cry me a river.

Fine I will get to my point smarty pants. Like I said I am single and I love sleeping alone on my bed. I guess different people sleep in different postures. Some of the most commonly used postures are the fisher dude – one arm out of the bed as if holding a fishing pole. The Greek play tragedy stricken hero – with one arm resting dramatically across the forehead. The ballerina – left feet resting on the right thigh (or vice versa) and one arm over the head post. The hung-over schmuck – face planted on the floor with one foot on the bed. Personally I prefer the runner. I sleep on one side, legs wide apart like when you are running. Quite comfortable. Fair warning though, the runner may not be quite as comfortable if you are a guy and it’s a cold night; it is not very accommodating to certain body parts. Back to my original point. I keep losing the plot during these monologues you know. Now I know how Ted Mosby from how I met your mother feels. Anyways, about my paranoia, I am paranoid about people touching my feet when I am sleeping. Never wake someone up by touching their feet. I mean who does that? Unless you are revenge seeking dead hooker ghost or a blood thirsty hack saw murderer. Not that I believe in dead hooker ghosts. But the latter is quite possible. And no cold blooded murderer would kill you when you are sleeping. Hollywood has taught me that they always have the courtesy to wake you up. My point is, my roommate tried to wake me up by touching my feet tonight and I acted reflexively in my sleep and kicked her in the face. Hard. She ricocheted off and hit the wall and broke her nose.

The medics are tending to her as I type this. She is giving me these angry glares in-between the astringent solution induced cries of agony. I must say with all that blood and the weird nose, she does look quite scary and threatening. I should probably not be typing this now but I have always been berated for my lack of knack and tasteless behavior. No wonder. I think I might have some priority issues. And attention deficiency. Maybe a few other issues too. Well they want to take her to the hospital. Oh there she is biding me farewell. Inappropriately vulgar hand gesture to say bye, but I think she might be a little upset. Well I guess I better go with her. But remember. Never. Never ever wake up somebody by touching their feet. Ever.



---End Of Story---
