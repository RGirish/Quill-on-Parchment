Night was falling, thick and fast. She looked up in search of the moon; her only solace in an otherwise dark world. Immersed in her thoughts, she walked on fervently hoping that the next turn will lead her to a lane with functioning street lights. Fear was her only companion that lonely night. The darkness engulfed her as a slight shiver went down her spine. Too many years spent in this city had taught her lessons. The night was not for the faint-hearted. Her head held high, she walked on with a sense of purpose which belied the fears within. A patch of light from the pavement threw out two shadows. She picked hers out but panic struck on seeing another. Too close for comfort, she had missed her silent companion. Fear gripped her insides as her mind was thrown into an ocean of turmoil. Ever so slightly, she quickened her pace but the shadow kept up. How she longed for the phone she had unceremoniously locked away; all for a bit of peace and quietude. With not a soul on the street, she knew that this was the one moment she had been warned about all her life. At night, the streets have a life of their own. Eerie and sly; women were mocked, teased and raped.

She was told "You are a girl. The night is not for you. There are men out there who will exploit you".

She never paid heed. To her, the silence of the night held within it a depth which penetrated her soul. She embraced the dark lanes of her neighborhood. Frightening as they may seem, she was drawn to them like a moth to light; a bee to a flower. But tonight, tonight was different. As the gap between the shadows fell away, she doubted her courage. Her fierce belief in a society so depraved ebbed away. Out of the corner of her eye, she saw movement. A hand extended, the shadow followed suit. An inexplicable pull tugged at her mind as her body slowed involuntarily. Was she crazy? There she was, in the dead of the night with a stranger behind her and she was slowing down! She heard a low moan and fear prickled at her senses. Run! Her senses screamed. Turn around, her instinct begged.

In that split second that the hand reached out to her, she whirled around; arms raised, poised to strike. She was left immobile. Staggering forward, arms out stretched with a bloodied patch over the eye a man stood; demented. A soul more tortured, one could not have seen. A pitiful cry escaped his lips as he crumpled in front of her. As she instinctively reached out to him, caution spoke to her through her mother. What if it was a plot? What if it was staged, to trap her? What if his cronies caught up with her? But that was fear speaking. Her conscience asked, "What if it was not? Would you let a man die?"

With a prayer on her lips, she pulled him up and hoisted him onto the pavement. She sped back home, jumped up the wall, scrambled up the ladder she had rolled out of her window and ran into her room. Grabbing her entire stash of food from under her bed with a bottle of water in hand, she ran down the street towards the man. Those dead eyes sensed compassion and glimmered with hope. Food was consumed on the off-chance that someone threw out anything edible. Afraid it might disappear; the man held onto every morsel and wiped the remnants clean. Fear had no meaning, mused the girl as she sat next to him. A stranger to kindness, he was. A stranger to him, she was. Will she ever forget that night? The night she took a leap of faith. The night it paid off. The world has plenty of good. You only have to believe.
