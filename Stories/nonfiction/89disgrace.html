It was sunshine and I woke up feeling something soft, snuggling near me- Something heavy and living tugging at my sheet. Squinting, I tried to focus at the object. In a slow transition from blur to focus I saw my cat looking up at me.

‘Meow.’

As I sat up cross-legged it craned its neck to my calf and I felt its body vibrate as would a soft motor. Gingerly I petted it as it purred louder. However, what followed was not cute and cuddly; though I liked animals I never loved them. They only fascinated me. Being averse to the way of a cats’ lick-cleansing I yanked my bed sheet out and sent it away from my bed calling it away as I put my bed sheet into the washing machine.

This cat, I should tell you is a disgrace to the animal kingdom. It would give the whole world, why, a juicy mouse for a plate of wheat dosa, torn into pieces and soaked in milk and cream. One should see it salivating and scampering to and fro from the kitchen to the side yard yearning my mother to give it. Pure Adorable. Things don’t stop there. It wants one of us to sit beside it while it ate. The arrangement, as is generally contrived, is not out of an unfathomable love but a security plea. The cat, turned out, was terrified of another dense black, stocky and I should tell drop dead handsome, cat: the alpha male of the area. The antagonist, donning white socks turned up once a week mauled the cat, licked clean its food and ran away.

My disgrace hadn’t jumped out of heaven either. It would go meowing around our street dog when it was inside the compound as strict orders, not to harm the cat, was drummed into its ears. No conundrum, it played god inside the perimeter. Nevertheless once outside, the dog never missed an opportunity to get back and the shrewd cat was always watchful.

This was not our first experience with cats and several other interesting quirks are worth mentioning. This disgrace had no name. We tried to come up with a really grand name like Alexander or Thomas or Sundarapandian in contrast to its dumb looks and habits (It would spend hours gazing at a lizard behind the window panel and jump at it at regular intervals only to hit against and fall back and resume with it again. Every day.) We tried calling it Thomas, but it only responded to the sound of the opening of the latch of the side yard and my mother’s ‘Odiya pussy’ (‘Come running pussy’). The previous cat was christened ‘Whiny’ as it was an irresolute whiner while the one before it was Kuka, short for Kunda Kalutha, Tamil for fat donkey! One was Poppy and the ancestor of all had a grand name: (Drum roll) Aachichipopochi- Poppachichiaa. Well it’s more like the name and the name again said backwards. Not exactly though. Ah, come on it’s a cat!

On one occasion, when we had guests and the house was full, my disgrace acted quite strange. It wouldn’t come into the house and it constantly meowed and jumped onto the compound wall intending us to do follow it. Puzzled we started thinking seriously if it was indeed trying to tell us something. Alas, after almost half an hour of brow-shrinking, it turned out that the fishmonger was in the compound opposite to us and it wanted a grab. Very intelligent. This was when my dad reminded me that animals were animals and they only obeyed instincts. And when I quoted stories from the popular Reader’s Digest and other excerpts, where animals displayed remarkable sense, he dismissed them as mere coincidence.

My brother, though, would have begged to differ with his new and Internet-oriented Knowledge. Having an average, not-so-inquisitive mind I went away a little puzzled though I leaned more towards my dad’s theory. May be if I had a dog the arguments would have differed.

However, in ways more than one, they carry a striking resemblance to humans. For instance, the routine of courtship. The tomcat starting from the grand ancestor till my current disgrace would start with going after a she-cat and go on to meekly let it feed on its supper till one day the she-cat would thrash the tomcat and become the mistress. Later the kittens usually turn up in some corner of the house. We lost the ancestor and would have lost Thomas as well if not for our timely alertness and past experience. Summarized, the male loses its life when the female slinks in. Maybe the above sentence fits all the other pronouns as well! And one more thing, we saw a streak of the In-law relationship in us when our disgrace brought along its girl.

Also, maybe animals make such good companions only because they are oblivious to the stark and disliked human errs. A mention of which, I presume, is not needed.

And, now I should leave! I can hear my cat’s agonizing cry. Behold! Mr. Handsome has returned.
