It was one breezy evening and I was reading ‘Eldest’ by Paolini. I had just padlocked the doors behind my parents as they ambled out for an evening stroll when all of a sudden, I heard batons and guava sticks being beaten against followed by screams and shrieks affront my house. In a rush I put the book aside and went out. My neighbour, with a baton in his hand, was running frantically tracing a serpentine path- SMACK! Another SMACK! - it was a snake. By the time, I opened the gate and rushed out into the street, the snake could in the maximum writhe. It was glued to the place with its sinews, flesh and blood that smeared as a paste.

My neighbour had spotted the snake near the entrance to his house. So unfortunate! Intending for the safer aspect, he wanted to kill it. The act is justified that there were numerous kids playing about and their safety lied in jeopardy; nevertheless, what of the snake?

Not that it stopped with the thrashing, but a group of onlookers gathered around to see it struggle- A mob ranging from toddlers to mid-fifties gathered around. As for the snake it writhed in pain all the while. As it writhed, the crowd cheered on yelling that the snake was doing a break dance and several other rude jibes followed. To stop it from squirming, my neighbour hit it again and it wriggled more from pain. Spontaneously, the crowd cheered and raised a louder tumult. The snake opened its mouth and looked around imploringly as if asking the crowd to relent. The crowd, on the other hand became more and more exuberant indulging into deeper pleasure at the agony of the creature. It was heart achingly pathetic. The snake desperate, gasping for air, died down after a few moments and laid still. The crowd after disposing the snake, dispersed after an extended gossip.

This incident happened sometime in my twelfth summer holidays. However, the incident that moved me to write not happened, but a few days ago.

It was nine in the morning and there was the usual power cut. I opened the window that some light flooded in. As I glanced out into my backyard, I saw quite a long snake slithering blissfully into the motor room. Not much was needed to flood my backyard with enough able-bodied men holding thrashing weapons- A gasp, fear and a few frantic phone calls sufficed. They carefully strategized their positions: two in the front, two in the rear and two above, perching in the compound wall. The ones above prodded the snake with a long stick. There was not a movement. The atmosphere had mixed emotions of excitement and caution. They prodded it again. The snake flew out as if to scare people away and went back in. They prodded it a third time. This time, it came out half-way and went back in a little subdued. May be it knew that it had no way out. For the last time, as one last desperate attempt to hold on to life, it slithered out frantically, but- SMACK! It was a one-shot victory for the men. The snake stopped dead in its tracks. Successive thrashes made it immovable. Then, to the spectacle of the crowd that had gathered, it was carried and flung into the vacant plot.

“I am sure; it’s the same snake that I saw the other day”

“Ditto” and several other housewife-ish talks arose. A mad spell of rain and wind dispersed the crowd and washed away the blood pools left by the snake.

Despite the fact, that snakes were cursed by God to eat dust and be hated by man, it is pathetic to see it get thrashed. Do not we humans seem to exercise an unfair advantage over it? It was we who actually moved deeper and deeper into the forestlands and we call the snake a stray!

True, humans have a better stand in God’s creations than the snake having been molded into his own image.

Things were and are meant to be this way. This is neither a question nor awakening; but mere expression of thoughts.
