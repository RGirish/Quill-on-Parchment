As I embark on my journey to adulthood, I look upon my years as a child, as a teenager, as a student- as a person with many roles- with a sense of both pride and fear, for I will be leaving behind everything that I have been familiar with for as long as I remember. I will be leaving the comfort of my home for a dorm room. I will be leaving behind my family for a community. I will be walking away from a city with a lifetime of memories, to a place that is my new reality.

It is a daunting task, to leave behind certainty for the foggy tomorrow, turning the first page to the beginning of a new chapter. Especially for a girl who is seventeen going on eighteen, who has lived vicariously through her books and her imagination. The world is not only her oyster, it is also her canvas, her clay, to paint on or to mould the way she sees fit.

In this instance, I fancy myself that girl; the one who views the world with an inquisitive eye, plucking stories out of mid air whilst going through every opportune door. This new reality, this new adventure that I am to set upon will be where I start life anew, with the memories of my old one shaping it and setting a path that is sure to be filled with potholes that I would have to stumble upon, break a few bones as a consequence, maybe. But it will also be my learning platform, as I progress each year towards the successful individual I strive to be.

There will be moments of weakness, moments of grave error, but to overcome these will all be part of the journey. Moments that I will look upon a number of years from now with the same pride I feel now, minus the fear. These moments will be the stories that I will tell one day, maybe sitting around a fire or at a bar, reminiscing with the people who lived them along with me and those who didn’t.

But for all of this to happen, I need to move forward courageously at this moment, as I look back one more time at my past, at the life I have lived- however dire it may seem. I need to take the first step that will lead to what I hope and believe to be a future of assured happiness and success; even if it is one that comes with its own mess of problems. For all problems are created to be solved, just like all mistakes are made to learn not to repeat them.

I am not going to lie, for I consider it futile to lie to oneself at moments like these, when I say that it will be hard to find, what I know to be the inborn bravery, to take this step. But to overcome the obstacles is what life is mostly about. (The rest is to bask in the glory of such victories, for what is life without some respite from hindrances?)

I believe that I ought to go about life, relishing the journey, rather than worrying about the destination. But there’s no objection on fantasising luxuries to be earned along the way, is there? I find myself lost at moments, struggling to find meaning to what I am doing and I hope that when I start this new chapter that I find the beginnings of the answer that I seem to be looking for.

I yearn to find the missing parts that will make up the rest of my life, filling up blanks so to speak. I am excited, anticipating every new puzzle piece I will uncover. The kind of excitement that only a naive girl like me could feel at such a defining moment because despite my big words, I am still a girl, learning my place in this vast, vast world. And I know only a little more than nothing. But is it not this kind of excitement that fuels dreams that one hopes to turn true?

At this interlude between my growth from a child to an adult, I ponder on these thoughts. I wonder if I am making the right decisions, wonder if I my expectations are too high, wonder if I am too green for what is yet to come. It’s this period in time which frustrates me, the waiting period as my fears and anxiety fosters in my heart, making me question and worry about the future. So, I confess that while I am as excited as the next person, I am equally frightened. I can only hope to find the means to quell these fears by the time this period of respite is over and get ready for battle in my helm and armour.

I’d like to conclude by saying this: While all these years have been a struggle, and a mighty one at that, where there have been extremely dark moments, there have been good ones too and lessons learnt. And when I look back I will remember both the good and bad and will be glad for both, for it helped me develop as a person.

And what I hope to achieve in the years to come is the same thing, moments I can look back at and be glad for. Hope to have changed a great deal, as well, preferably for the better. But what I hope for the most, is for me to not have any regrets.

When I look back, I want to say ‘I did this. It was not entirely thought through and in the long run, it was a bad decision. But I did it and I stick with my choice, because I took a chance, took a risk; even if I played the wrong card all that matters in the end is that I played and I learnt not to make the same mistake the next time.’

(As theatrics go, I find comfort that I have it in me to exaggerate this to the maximum extent possible. It gives me a talent and that gives me something to be proud of)
