To all those budding graduates, this movie might just not be an inspiration but the real face of the present day’s competition. With the promos and the music that had already caught the pulse of the audience, the movie doesn’t fail to impress you.

Dhanush has sealed a place for himself in the industry after the Nayandi debacle. The movie starts off with Raghvaran (Dhanush) being an engineer who never finds a suitable job for himself who has a employed brother which explains his position in the family and is often shown being blasted by his dad. Shamuthrakani who portrays this role has justified it to a great extent.

The first half of the movie is on a lighter tone. It revolves around how Raghuvaran spends a jobless life. Even after realizing the state in which he is, the positivity of the character is what drives you through the first 60 mins of the movie. Some great scenes between Dhanush and Sarayana(mom) and the everlasting mother sentiment would touch every kid. The twist in the interval which might actually make you sob a lot.

The actual story beings in the second half. A well-wisher of Dhanush gets him the job that he wants. A construction business, a big contract and it gets much filmier beyond this. The scene between Dhanush and the villain looked much more than Tom and Jerry. That 2 minute punch dialog that he spoke looked resplendent and was hardly audible with whistles and claps in the theatre.

Vivek has pulled of his small role remarkably well with his one liners.

The person that fails to make an impression in the movie and this review is Amala Paul. Mynaa would probably be the only film where she actually has acted. Amala has followed the same set of stereotypical roles for quite a few films.

Coming to the crew, the debut director, Velraj who has an experience of almost 15 years in this industry as a cinematographer has been lauded with praise for his outstanding work.

An Anirudh Ravichander Musical, people hardly do listen to the songs before praising it. With going a step ahead with the background score, the music is very ideal for a very commercial entertainer like VIP.

Verdict: Dhanush has passed the VIP test. With excellent screen play and direction, this movie might just be a motivation for the 5 lakh engineers that pass out every year. A perfectly mixed film with right amount of Masala and emotions.
