I am going to talk about the nuWho (new who) alone, for I haven't seen the classic yet. Those who don't know, the Doctor Who series was brought back to life in the year 2005 and from there on it is known as the nuWho. Before I jump into the depths of my admiration for this show, I would like to point out that I haven't watched a lot of science fiction shows. So I am treating the genre of this series as drama instead of science fiction. This is the 1st series in a long time I picked myself, instead of someone suggesting it to me. The reason behind it was not the fact that people were talking about it a lot, but just the Tardis design and the color which I came across thanks to Tumblr.

What is it really about?

The show revolves around The Doctor. Yes, we call him The Doctor, simply because he doesn't reveal his name and asks to be called The Doctor. He isn't your everyday doctor with a stethoscope around his neck. He is The Doctor, the last of the Time Lords, travelling around the length and breadth of space and time in his police box looking time machine/space ship called the Tardis (Time and relative dimension in space). Plus he is also 900 something years old. He travels around like a nomad with a companion. The companions are usually female human beings but there have been male companions. The companions change from time to time, because some choose to stay behind, while others don't really have a choice. The Doctor so just travels around in his Tardis with a companion, that's all the show is about. And also he manages to save the earth and the whole universe from time to time.

Why is it such a big thing?

People who haven't really watched the show, just bits and pieces, complain that it is just silly. A alien using a magic wand to fix everything and flying a phone box sounds just silly. And I pity all those people. Doctor Who takes the art of storytelling to a whole new level. There is a reason I didn't want to put this series in the Science-Fiction genre, because it's not always about sci-fi. It's much more than that, transcends and wets its feet in a lot of different genre. Also, it's labelled as a kids show, but truth be said it is for the kid in you. There is a story somewhere in the pile of 7 seasons (till date) for you. And also the cast is spot on, even though its really big. Add to that the theme music composed for different doctors and the companions are absolute gold (Pun intended).

Love and Hate...

I love the show, every much. I am one of the few lucky persons, I will get back to that. There is one thing about The Doctor, I haven't told you yet. The Doctor doesn't die, he regenerates. He changes his face, his personality and his quirks. When the show is rebooted, we start the ride with the 9th doctor. Fan girls can't handle this change, and some still are hung up on the 10th, some on the 11th. Then there is the classic Moffat vs Davies debate. Russel T Davies was the show runner till the 4th season and Moffat took his reins after that. People and Whovians on the internet seem to hate Moffat's era. But I guess that's just a fraction of the fans. There were bad episodes on both the sides, and there is no hiding it. Both of them seem to have forgotten logic at times and both of them have given us wonderful episodes. Russel T Davies tried to tug at your heart. His scripts were earth centric and had to do with the Doctor saving it and seeing the good in humanity. While Moffat wanted to scratch your brains and wanted to have you thinking. His stories were time and Doctor centric. People usually take sides- Russell T Davies or Moffat, The 10th or The 11th. And this is where I am lucky. I have been able to equally like both the eras and all the Doctor's. And even there was a point I thought I was watching two different shows. There are few bad episodes I hate, but that's what you expect when a show runs for 8 seasons. But everyone has their own opinions and the show is not really in any danger. It did lose some fans because of the 7th season, I am sure it will gain more.

Is this the show for you?

This show is not for everyone. If you want to know if this show is for you, then please try out Blink (S3E10). If you loved it, then the chances are that this show is for you. I had fun and the show pushed me into the pond of feels from time to time. It was an absolute roller coaster the past month and a week, where I zipped past 7 seasons of this great, fantastic, brilliant piece of entertainment. I am so excited for the next season.
