There are a ton of TV shows which really contain slapstick comedy or boring melodrama and nothing more. These shows stay on the air for 7 to 10 seasons and we really wonder why. But a TV show as awesome as Firefly getting cancelled after a mere 14 episodes, is nothing but a real shame. It kind of gets me questioning what’s wrong with people. That’s a question I won’t be pondering upon on this post, but another question “What makes Firefly really soar in its short lived life?” is what I will be pondering on. I lied to a friend of mine that I watched it when I didn’t. I wish I had watched this sooner. Better late than never I guess. I would like to thank that friend of mine for giving me something as good as this.

What is it really about?

The show is set in a post war era, where a China-American power block called the Alliance have won. The Alliance consists of what is known as the central planets, the planets which is known to prosper. These planets have better living standards. And there are other planets and moons which are habitable but are not as advanced as the central planets. In this era, there exists a small transport ship known as ‘Serenity’, captained by Malcom Reynolds, who does some honest and not so legal jobs to keep his ship flying. His crew consists of Zoe – the second in command, Wash –the pilot with the funny bone (Husband of Zoe), Jayne – the all muscles no brain man, Kaylee – the ever smiling mechanic. They have a registered companion on board, Inara. Registered companions are respectable, highly trained, physiatrist and pleasure workers. A shepherd, named Book, boards and travels with this crew. What happens when a Doctor, Simon and his deranged physic sister, River who is hunted by the Alliance boards the ship forms the core of this franchise. So this is essentially a show about 9 different people stuck together.

Why is it so likeable and loved?

Some shows like Sherlock is pure class, each and every frame oozes it. But firefly doesn’t do that. In fact it’s strengthen likes in its simplicity. How many sci-fi space themed shows is bold enough not to feature aliens? I can only think of this show. It doesn’t try to do anything fancy and tries to keep it simple and true. It tries to unify old western cultures with that of space fantasy. And it succeeds greatly. The underlying meaning is that even when technology advances, the existing problems just mutilates itself to the advances. And it is showcased in a very simplistic, yet in the most entertaining way possible. The cast is perfect in its own way. I really thought Nathan Fillon’s born to play role was Castle, but I had to change my mind after watching Firefly. He is just pure class and a natural to play Captain Mal. He holds the crew and the franchise together. Summer Glau is given the toughest role to play and she plays it to near perfection. The rest of the cast plays it part to its very best. But the true reason why it’s so lovable is the sassy and witty dialogues. Trust me, when you read the quotes after watching it, you will be smiling like a child. Perfect cast, simple engaging script and witty dialogues, what else do you need?

Really makes me sad….

Yes, it pains me to realize that 14 episodes and a movie is all I get. Though the movie is really top notch, it fails to answer few questions. Questions that can be answered very efficiently through hour long shows instead of a single two hour long movie. Firefly had so much potential that it would easily have been the best show out there. In its own way, it had everything from comedy to melodrama. If I had a shooting star to wish upon, I would wish for the continuation of this franchise. If I were to make a wish every time I blow candles on my birthday, it would be for this franchise.

Is this show for you?

If you are a fan of the sci-fi genre and haven’t watched this, please drop everything you are doing and try to watch this. I can honestly say you won’t regret this. If you are a not a fan of sci-fi genre, give this a try, really give this a try. Just because it came out in 2002, don’t think it is outdated. The theme is universal and timeless. This is a true television classic, the one you shouldn't miss. Or at least for the brilliance of Nathan Fillon, this is a must watch. Show like this is a ship and…..
