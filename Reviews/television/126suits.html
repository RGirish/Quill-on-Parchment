Let me start off by how I was introduced to this show- Suits. It was some time since my show-suggestion-agent pointed his/her finger at any show and called it guilty of being awesome. And as I was already invested in Doctor Who, I didn't really give it a lot of thought to find another show by myself. But what intrigued me was the attention this show was getting from my seniors. So I had to put this on the top of the list past GoT, MAoS and Fargo. Lets get into my thoughts now.

What this show is really about ?

Suits, falls under workplace drama and the profession in question happens to be the Legal profession. Getting to the specifics, this show is really about a young man, Mike Ross who never has gone to a law school talk his way into being the associate of one of the most prolific lawyers, Harvey Specter in NY. Harvey hires Mike despite knowing the truth, because Mike is such a genius. Anyone who has photographic memory coupled with a sharp mind can be called a genius . So, the show is about this duo, the experienced and the young, going about their daily businesses and the other things that happen to them. In short we have a lawyer who doesn't want to lose each and every time and his associate with a big secret who wants him to win each and every time.

What makes this show click?

Having watched three seasons of this show, I can see why people like it. The only other show regarding lawyers I have seen is Boston Legal, and I loved that for Alan Shore. Same thing here, I love this show for Harvey and Mike but there is no more to this than that alone. There James Spader carried Boston Legal on his shoulders and maybe got a little help from William Shatner. But here, its not that case. The whole cast makes the show work and allows it to shine. From Gina Torres as Jessica Pearson to Rick Hoffman as Louis Litt, they all stand out as individual characters and are given enough space. The show doesn't deal with workplace experiences alone but looks into their lives as well. The women in this show, all have been given pretty strong roles to play and that is refreshing to see. I am saying that for I didn't expect it when I first started the show. I thought it was just going to be like a Yoda-Luke kinda thing but I was wrong. I am glad I was wrong. Character development is one solid reason for the success of this show. The next reason is the dialogues. This is a show about lawyers,so it is be expected that the dialogues are razor sharp when they are needed to be and that's what we are treated to. Sprinkled with little humor and an never-ending number of movie references, one couldn't ask for more. The last reason is a unique one that I haven't seen in other shows. The dynamic duo seems to screw themselves up more than any other real bad guy doing that. They make the mistakes and they fix it and how they do it, is what makes the show really click.

My Conclusion

The 1st season was setting me up, the 2nd was picking up pace while the 3rd just went on a roller coaster ride. The beauty of the show is, I knew what was going to happen but how it happened is what blew me away. One of the wittiest shows I have seen and I could recommend it to anyone who as an appetite for a proper show which keeps its viewers on the edge with words.
