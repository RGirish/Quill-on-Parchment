Some shows target a specific audience, like Teen Wolf was clearly made for teens in mind. Some shows take up its genre very seriously, like Grey's Anatomy making sure drama takes center stage. These two methods are considered to be the 'Safe bet' by the producers. But rarely a show sometimes truly tries to be more than it meets the eye. Be like a cake with different layers of flavors instead of being spectacularly singled flavored. That's something difficult, for there is a risk of not getting the flavors right and also the proportion matters. And that's exactly what Castle does.

What is it all about?

It is about a novelist, Richard Castle (Nathan Fillion) shadowing a female homicide detective, Kate Beckett (Stana Katic) for research and inspiration. Rick has a teenage daughter, Alexis (Molly Qunin) and also it is to be noted that his mother, Martha lives along with the two. On the other side, Ryan and Esposito form the rest of the team along with Castle and Kate. The show's anchor point is Beckett's mother, Johanna Beckett's murder case.

What flavors are we talking about?

For starters Castle's primary genre is 'crime'. I can't say it to be a disguise or a cloak worn. The mysteries are generally well scripted and all in all justice is done. But it will be a letdown for true crime loving fans who expect it to be something like Sherlock or even CSI. I say this because some episodes, it is predictable and in some it tries a little too hard. Episodes such as this are very less, after all there are bound to a few bad episodes when a TV show runs for six seasons. If there ever was a chink in its armor, then this is it. The next big flavor is the 'romance'. Romance in this TV show is realistic to a large extent. It's rare to see such chemistry between the lead pair where solving murders is the priority. Only other show that has this, is Sherlock. It’s not the lovey-dovey teen romance we are talking about. The chemistry and romance is much matured and yet refreshing. The next flavor that makes a difference is 'Comedy'. Seriously, Castle is one of the funniest non-sitcom show, I have ever seen. It might even give a run for some sitcom shows. The key here is that the writers don't try to overdo it. Then there is a subtle flavor of “Parent Child bonding". I don't want to really say much about and spoil it. The writers also embrace sci-fi, supernatural and pop-culture themes from time to time.

What else makes it click other than the 'flavors'?

After watching a fair number of TV shows and movies, I have come to a conclusion. For movie to excel, it needs to make sure the script is its number one priority followed by the cast. But for a TV show, the casting has to be perfect and has to compliment the script. TV shows such as Sherlock and Big Bang Theory have managed to hit the bull's eye as far as casting goes. And Castle always manages to get on the list. Nathan Fillion is the perfect casting for a novelist who is like nine year old on a sugar rush. He is such a delight and a natural. It's like he was born for this role. Stana Katic also plays the complex and multi-layered role of Kate Beckett to perfection. The rest of the cast just falls on to its place, like a jig saw puzzle. Sound track by Robert Duncan is pleasing. Other aspects are all above the mark and that's what makes Castle a delight.

Is this show for you?

This show is not for people who want something serious like Hannibal. And also this is not for people who want shows that are intense like CSI. This is a blend of various things. As a downside to that, it may or may not work. As much as I know, it has worked for many, many people I know. If you are going to watch it as a crime show, you are likely to be left with a little, minute disappointment. But if you are going to watch it just as anything else, you will have a good time.
