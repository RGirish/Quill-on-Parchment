Panting horse runs insane

Bearing the King’s cry

My queen sits there ruffling its mane

Of course she’s not shy!


As the poor thing carries us

there wails an alarm

The audio of the wild now shuts

Blown off like storm


The ‘Khaitan’ spins faster than ever

Symbolizing my brain

Searching for my horse and ‘HER’

I dive back into my dream


But the villain with numbered pox (cellphone)

Trumpets his junk soon

The snooze-mode like wily fox

Breaks my honeymoon


I wish I had the Nokia prez

Tied near my bed

To adorn his Finn night dress

With stinky stains of red


Hopping out of my cot I greet Mr. Col Gates

This rich man makes me smile they say, who now I really hate


I pity my swollen face with grace

As I wield the toothbrush

My cheek cells wake on water’s pace

Omg! The morning-rush!


I now see my queen again

And my faithful colt

Then I get an eye-pain

Only to see my washroom door-bolt!


Getting out of the conference room, I

switch on the heater

Reaching my study room

I find my book from the scatter


With solemn grit I wake up the big rogue

Who calls himself ‘Inorganic Chemistry’

only to see the prologue

and the author’s history!


Damning for my time waste,

I reach my correct page

I start getting the ‘Chemisty-taste’

Murmuring like a sage


All izz well till I hear a neigh

as I run thru the words

the sentence now airs from the sky

Oh, I can see many swords!


It’s war-time for my state

which I tell myself to win

I fluctuate between my two states

fighting my eyelid battle like throat of pin.


My queen sees me desperately as war rages by

"Chlorine has high electron-affinity' is my only reply


I gaze at the bond structure

which transforms into shields

of warriors like H2,O2

fighting within their ‘beaker-batttlefield’


Comes the chain process to my rescue

as my reactant warriors shoot the product guys

with arrows of catalytic hues


As I give my final blow

to my foe

time for hero’s speech

‘The bond inert but so strong’

I say stretching the bow ‘reducing’ him like leech


But my world changes like Sully’s

when I see my tea-cup alias the ‘victory wine’

the sky still dark apparently

as the Timex makes a straight line

(6 o’ clock)


AS I start thinking about the day’s test

A cold wave floods my spine

“I need some rest” I say to my queen

as my eyelashes entwine


But the torture wont stop I guess

till I gain my sense

Suddenly my phone hisses

and displays a sentence!


“Hey! No school, thanx to the showers”

“The test postponed for good”

I erupt with all my power

as the Lake. Joy floods...


I proclaim this mail to my (actual) people (parents)

and rush to see my queen

The cash of time ,now ,very ample

with skies of eternal sheen!


Goes on and on the...the bashes

ending the clash of my eyelashes!!!!



---End Of Poem---
