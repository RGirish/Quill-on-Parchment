Sitting near the window sill

Peeping the world through the window,

That alone is left in my control

Rest expecting others

For every action of mine.


Sitting near the window sill

In my new wheel chair

Just two days old

The beginning of a new era

After the non- stop movements


Gone are the days

Of my trekking camp

And the long walks

My love for ever

To be on foot


Never preferred an auto

For walking the way makes me happier

Even today, never prefers an auto

For climbing with the help of a walker

Is equally painful.



This sudden illness

Rather the slow incoming of illness

Paving the way for my dependence

Was irresistible

As no test can spot the cause.


It all started with a small fracture

A fracture in right foot,

But was wrong for my future

Arresting the free bird on a wheel chair

Allowed only to muse on past.


I feel subdued

I feel arrested

Time never moves

Everything is still

I have to push it.


God is generous

He never tested me long

Those who silently

Bear His yoke also serve Him

I was finally redeemed



A small tumour

Opened the way

For my boom

A small diagnosis

Opened the way for my resurrection.


Yes, I am back

I am once again

Brought back to life

To stand on my own feet,

A miracle by the heaven.


I mingle in this world

Fast moving world

With slow steady pick up

To my daily chore

And involve in all activities.


Now I know the value of life,

The mirth of getting life back,

I wished for one day free walk,

He blessed me free movement forever.

I relish his blessings!



Now viewing through the window

Waiting for my son’s return from school

I muse over the past

The cloudy part of my life

Now brightened by Him


No time to stand and muse

Have to prepare Tiffin

And prepare tea for the evening.

All is over like a dream

And I am back with His grace.


Back to life,

Back to independence,

Back to mirth,

Back to my routine,

The joyous of life.



---End Of Poem---
