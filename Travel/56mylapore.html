Have you ever decided to get lost? Have you ever just gotten on a random bus and loosen up yourself, just like that! Well this is about one such incident when my friends and I “got lost”.

It all started when we (four of us) bunked college and wandered around Tambaram bus stand thinking where to go next. It was soon declared that we would hop onto the very next bus that left the depot. There it was, the deluxe 21G that left to Mandaveli.

It took almost an hour for the bus to steer through the traffic to Mandaveli depot. We got down and stared at the bus for a couple of minutes, not knowing what to do. After the awakening by a deathly honk of another bus leaving the depot, we started walking towards Ramakrishna matt.

NOTE: When you have no clue what to do, just get into random AIR- CONDITIONED shops and pretend like you are going to buy something.

After an exuberant drama of getting our “nephew” a dress, at kidzon and of course beating the heat with the free air conditioner, we were forced to leave by their cold stares of ‘you’re not going to buy anything , are you?’

As unexpected, Ramakrishna matt was closed. So we decided to eat lunch instead. Mylapore has a lot of food places. Starting for a very huge saravaa bhavan to small mess’, you name it you got it. We decided to hog at Vasanta bhavan opposite to kabali temple kolam, south mada street and obviously no meal is complete without sweet saunf (fennel).

We took a big walk around kabali kolam and ended up drinking goli soda. If you are anywhere near mylapore, do ask for kalahasthi store and try the rose milk. This shop has been there for generations and has the best rose milk.

After all the goli soda chilling out, we walked to Santhome church. It is definitely a master piece. At three in the afternoon, the sun was directly opposite to the beautifully constructed huge tinted windows located in the first floor, which casts a shadow of colours almost up to the altar.

The perfect day came to an end as we all just lay down under the lighthouse shadow at 3.30 on a really sunny hot day, licking popsicles.

So next time you are with your favourite bunch of idiots in Chennai, try to get lost and explore, for Chennai has so much to offer.
