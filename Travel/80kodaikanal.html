The bags were packed and kept in the verandah. My sister rushed in with excitement as I googled the song that I wanted from the third season Sherlock. December 1963!. With the Nokia Music voucher getting over the day after, I wanted to make the most of it. That downloaded and all set we got into the cab and reached the bus terminal with the usual jolts and squeezes. After waiting for some time amidst the sulphorous fumes and noises, we boarded the bus; to the destination that crowded in a good lot of memories and awaited a million more.

‘It is a place that has been good to them, as much as a member of the family. The idea of returning year after year to a single place appeals to Gogol deeply.’

As put in Namesake, the first novel by Pulitzer winner Jhumpa Lahiri, cannot explain better.

In a yearlong tight packed routine, as the last week of May of every year neared we became earnestly expectant. More of a family get-together than a sightseeing spree, a motley assortment bunched together: Government employees, Teachers, Professors, IT workers, house wives and as least in the hierarchy, students. We were all cousins, of the first and second round of my maternal side. The once in a year affair was when we got to see each other apart from marriages and special occasions; astringent routine called only for thus.

The place, a Christian fellowship, is currently run by a trust. Originally, the group of lodgements was places where the British military came to rest. The rooms we stayed were either the barracks or clubhouses and one cannot be sure as there remains no written record. It is also said that this was where injured soldiers were brought to convalesce. Thereafter it was acquired by a medical doctor, Ida Scudder, for the doctors at CMC (Christian Medical College and Hospital) Vellore, Tamil Nadu to come for their vacations.

‘One day in 1953, aged 82, she was at "Hilltop", her bungalow at Kodaikanal, which overlooked Vellore's Christian Medical College and its hospital, …’ is a mention of the place in the Wikipedia entry on her.

After a slew of encroachments and given-up-for-good law suits there remains the humble abode. The place has its own routine: A prayer in the morning, followed by breakfast, then lunch, tea at four, a get-together in the evening, a closing prayer and a dinner at seven. For the city dwellers where the daily nocturnal activities start at 8pm, there it ends by then. It becomes cold and all of us huddle into our rooms.

Not very populated and consequently not very technologized the only reprieve is the isolation from noise, traffic and as my mother puts it ‘Planning the day’s menu’. Being a low funded project, its chief aim is to cater to Christian missionaries. Hence it calls for frugal living. No electric devices were permitted, the food was vegetarian and simple and we had to do our own dishes. Needless to mention, bribes and blackmails ensued at each after-meal washing.

A tradition carried out faithfully for the past two generations, I well remember the first time I visited the place when I was 10. I can recollect that on seeing the pristine grass and flowers I ran along with my cousins to the front porch and we rolled like dogs in the wet grass. Out of continuity, yeah, but there are actual dogs now, big, healthy one’s of the cook that roll about and play in the grass. However, now we with the sense or bliss-snatcher, I am not sure, that came with age, refrain from doing the same unhygienic rolling. Nevertheless, the dogs are a pure adorable sight. The cook adopted a very simple way of nomenclature for her pets; a Jerry would bring all the dogs, a Puss all the cats and Gurgs, the geese. She has six dogs, six cats, geese and a few birds. These were her protection from monkeys, bisons, bears and thieves.

Whole, long hours we would sit under shades, lie about munching chocolates, licking ice creams, taking long walks or simply lying down on unused concrete blocks and looking above, at the shade of the Magnolia Crescent. And all these activities were accompanied constantly by my playlists. I believe that one can, like smells and sounds, associate songs with the time, period and location in one’s life. As much I can recollect, it was Evil Love by The Cast of Phineas and Ferb back in 2009 and December 1963 by Four seasons filled the slot in 2014. Meme references, absolutely joyous loitering and left out conversations and so much other that one can reflect and resound as Kurt Vonnegut wants everyone to, saying, ‘If this isn’t nice, what is?’

Slowly the day of our departure nears and the weeklong rest comes to an end. Though we love the pleasant climate and relish it we are people of the temperate and we start anticipating the sweltering heat and the sweat. With renewed vigor, we hit the plains with feet swollen and with skin tone shades darker, only to switch on the air-conditioner again.
